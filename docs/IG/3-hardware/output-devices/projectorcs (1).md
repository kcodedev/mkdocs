## _Liquid Crystal Display Projectors_ üíß

Liquid Crystal Display Projector is also known as **LCD projectors**. They are a type of video projector that uses Liquid Crystal Display panels to create and project an image onto a screen.



_**Step-by-step how LCD projectors work:**_

1. Starts off with a powerful lamp that creates white light.

2. It is sent to dichroic mirrors to splith the white light into seperate red, green and blue beams.

3. The color beams pass through their own liquid crystal display screens.

4. Each screens are made of thousands of tiny pixel that either block light or let it through. _This produces a **monochromatic** image._

5. The three color beams now carry its own grayscale image which pass through a prism to make a full color image.

6. Finally, the image passes through the projector lens onto a screen.

## _Digital Light Projectors_ üí°

**Digital Micromirror device (DMD)** are used by Digital Light Projectors (DLP).

_**Step-by-step how DLP work:**_

1. A bright white light passes through a color filter.

2. The white light is split into three primary colors: blue, red and green.

3. Electronic signals control each mirror on the DMD panel to tilt or adjust the lens so that different colors can be made.

4. Mirrors on the DMD panel switches on and off rapidly to control the brightness of each pixel. 

         (The faster the mirror is on, the brighter the pixel is)

5. Finally, the lens projects each pixel onto a screen, which creates a clear image.

![DLP color wheel front view](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/DLP_color_wheel_front_ACER.jpg/640px-DLP_color_wheel_front_ACER.jpg)
